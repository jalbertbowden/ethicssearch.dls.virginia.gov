  • EXECUTIVE AND LEGISLATIVE ACTIONS AND PROCUREMENT TRANSACTIONS

    The American Civil Liberties Union of Virginia lobbied the members of the General Assembly and the Governor’s office to support laws, regulations, policies, and procedures that seek to protect civil rights and civil liberties or codify protections against discrimination including legislation that sought to reform Virginia’s criminal, juvenile justice and mental health systems, dismantle the school-to-prison pipeline, ensure open and transparent government, ensure access to abortion and other reproductive health services, ensure access to government benefits without discrimination, remove laws and policies that grant public accommodations or other entities the right to deny services to individuals based on religious or moral belief, ensure proper oversight and accountability vis-a-vis the government’s use of surveillance and other technology, protect the right to vole and implement both the Free Exercise and Establishment clauses of he First Amendment. I asked legislators to support proposals to amend the Virginia Constitution to insert an affirmative right to vote, repeal the felony disenfranchisement provision and to enact no-excuse absentee voting laws.Additionally, I worked with legislators on amendments to bills seeking to ban female genital mutilation, wrote a letter to the Legislative Black Caucus about proposed voting rights amendments, and wrote letters to the Governor and talked with his staff about vetoes of legislation that would encourage censorship of books in public schools, authorize discrimination against LGBT people by certain businesses, require reporting of refugees resettled in Virginia, mandate local assumption of federal enforcement of immigration laws, impose arbitrary penalties on people publicizing protests, and impose new financial burdens on people awaiting trial.
