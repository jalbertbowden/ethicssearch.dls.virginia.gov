  • EXECUTIVE AND LEGISLATIVE ACTIONS AND PROCUREMENT TRANSACTIONS

    Attended meetings, political events and committee hearings. One of the main topics of conversation has been VSP salary and staffing issues. Both of the "money" committees, House Appropriations and Senate Finance, are developing their budget proposals and we remain confident that our problems will be addressed. SB805, sponsored by Senator Carrico will be reported out of Committee and forwarded to the Senate Finance Committee for its consideration. SB855, sponsored by Senator Tommy Norment, will be heard in Senate Transportation. This Bill that memorializes Trooper Chad Dermyer by naming a bridge in Newport News over I64 in his honor. A "companion" Bill, HB1405, sponsored by Delegate Keith Morris, will be heard in House Transportation either later this week or next week. HB1398, sponsored by Delegate Richard Bell, will be heard in the Criminal Law Subcommittee of House Courts. This Bill seeks to amend section 52-8.5 by making crimes committed against law-enforcement officers, firefighters or EMS personnel a "hate crime" for the purposes of reporting such acts to the Department for the collection and analysis of information regarding hate crimes and groups and individuals carrying out such acts. SB1250, sponsored by Senator Carrico, and HB2269, sponsored by Delegate Ron Villanueva (Chair of the House Transportation Committee) are companion Bills that authorizes the Superintendent to provide upon request, verification of the inspection status of a vehicle and to charge a reasonable fee for providing such information. This Bill is expected to generate approximately $100,000 a year for the Department. SB1250 is scheduled to be heard in Senate Transportation Wednesday. SB2269 is not yet scheduled. As a reminder "companion" bills are identical pieces of legislation it's just that one is introduced in the House and the other in the Senate.
